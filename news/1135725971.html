Robosapien
So as it turns out, my Girlfriend had decided to buy me a Robosapien for Christmas.



Its a wonderful peice of kit, i've had hours of fun with it already, although after you have been through all the functions about 50 times each you start to think "what else can it do...?".

This is where the book (The Robosapien Companion: Tips, Tricks, and Hacks by J. Samans) comes in really handy.

Not only did I learn about its special programming features and secrets, I learned about all sorts of hacks and mods I can do to my Robot.

I'm getting to the stage when i'm seriously considering doing the Robocamera mod, which simply requires a ZT-802 camera (about £18), and some spare time... They also recommend putting a tourch on the front to provide some extra light for the camera, I recon one of those new LED ones would go down a treat!

I was also looking at the USB-UIRT devices so I could simply control the Robosapien from my computer, allowing me to create and display all sorts of wonderful routines at the click of a button. The trouble is though, it costs $75 (about £44) to ship one to the UK. They are a useful little device, not only for this, but for lots of other IR devices too. This is know as the PC Control Mod.

It turns out you can also get some software that is related to the Robosapian, they are SapBench, Robosapien Dance Machine, Robots Dream and many more peice of Robosapien Control software. But I have yet to find a java/midp based one that will work on my mobile phone (Siemens CX65).

Last but not least! Probably the most important mod yet, the Volume control hack. Looks like a trip to Maplins for me.

Watch out for project Robosapien...

Some other websites to check out include:
Robosapien.tk
Robosapien1.com
TheRobosapien.com
Also check out Mark Craig's Robosapien Project, some cool stuff.
