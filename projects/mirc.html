mIRC Scripts

What is mIRC?
mIRC is very well described at mirc.com

All these scripts are designed for the latest version of mIRC, though most are backward compatable.
All the scripts were made by HM2K, though credit is given where appropreate.

Script Description
moo script v3.0 This script shows you the stats of your system to either you or everyone else that you are currently talking to on an irc channel or msg window, this script will show you the following information: moo.dll's version, your operating system, your uptime, your cpu, your memory usage, your screen settings, your connection, your network interfaces and your disk capacity. All in all, a useful tool. (no more moo.dll).
MyIP v0.13 Looks up and returns your ip address via sockets.
CJB Updater v0.16 This is an mIRC frontend of CJB.Netter for Windows.
This script allows you using mIRC sockets to connect to CJB.NET and change your IP or URL assigned to your account, and you can even make it update to your own IP automaticly when you connect to IRC. Enjoy!
Notepod for mIRC v1.7 And here it is, at last! NotePod is a channel definition script, similar in many respects to explain and fluxlearn and others, it works in almost the same way as notepod.tcl for eggdrop does, but better. e.g.:
?? HM2K
<Bot> HM2K == http://www.hm2k.org/
LNKs v0.1* Since smlnk is no longer in use, I decided to change the script to work with lnks.us run by netmunky.
Basicly what lnks does, is convert a long URL such as http://this.is.a.really.really.really.really.really.really.really.really.long.url/ (if it exists) and it returns with a user friendly url like: http://lnks.us/?XXXXXXXX for more information, visit http://lnks.us/?info. This script basicly a frontend to the site, allowing you to manually or automaticly convert the urls either way.
Google v1.5 Everyone knows what Google is right? Well this uses the "I'm feeling lucky" button on their site to find the best match for the text you input in from mirc and returns it back into mirc, using either !google for remote use or /google for local use depending on what you want.
Seen v1.02 Looking for someone? huh? Well, look no further, this script is designed to keep log of people quiting, parting, being kicked out of and changing their nick... It also tells you if they are still on irc, on a different channel or such, its basicly the easiest way to keep track of people.
Quotes v2.31 Do you ever find that while your on mIRC, that there are just quotes that you should log, or should store somewhere, maybe because they are funny, or embarrassing or similar, well now you can, with this quotes script, you can either add/find/remove/total quotes remotely, or locally, without any hassle. Never miss a quote again!
WinAmp v1.1* Ever wondered how people do that "* Nick is playing Artist - Song (length)" ? Well its simple, its usually done with an mIRC script and a WinAMP DDE plugin, but no more, this script looks up what song is currently playing in WinAMP and displays it in the channels you set in the script. May not work on slow computers. (dll included)
wget v0.3* A very useful script, its meant to be a (simple) version of wget for unix based systems, you can simply type: /wget [url] and it will download the file to your mirc downloads folder.
Commands Script v0.22* A script designed to replace the default mIRC popups. Has many features, such as mass deop etc.
UCC v0.01* Most of us use XE.com for our currancy conversion. This script will convert your currency for you via mIRC. eg:
<HM2K> !ucc 10 USD GBP
<BOT> UCC: 10.00 USD is 5.66271 GBP
Country v0.2* Returns the country code for any given domain name extention, e.g.:
<HM2K> !country .uk
<BOT> User: .uk is United Kingdom
Oper Commands v0.4* A simple script that is designed for opers, good with Unreal IRCD.
Protection Bot v0.1* This script was originally designed to get a grasp of sockets within mIRC and the way an IRCd responded to commands. Now it is developed into a fully functioning protection bot.
regmIRC v2.6* Keep forgetting that important mIRC registration code? Well here's your answer, simply type: /regmirc "nick" and mIRC will then register its self using your nick.

* In beta stages, or still under development.
