Nintendo Game Boy Advance SP: Tribal Edition

My girlfriend bought me a game boy for Christmas as I simply could not decide on anything else I would want. A few of the games I got were GTA: Advance, Scooby Doo, The Incredibles, and Zelda the minish cap.

The best features about the GBA:SP are the battery life, which is simply incredible, after about 10mins of charge it lasted most of xmas day, another great feature is the back light, this is the first game boy to have such a feature. Not only that but the GBA does have a fantastic big screen, your mobile phone looks pathetic compared. Also I must point out that the sound quality is very good, and very clear.

The one game I have been playing none stop since I received my GBA is Zelda: The minish cap. I loved playing the old game boy Zelda that was Zelda: Links awakening on my old game boy, but this is even better. I have got to a point so many times when I think I simply cannot go on any further, and I will be literally trying EVERYTHING to get further, and finally I find it.

If your like me you may find this useful: http://www.neoseeker.com/Games/Products/GBA/zelda_minish_cap/faqs.html (I have mirrored the one I used at certain points for hints here)

However now I am at that point again, but this time I have 999 rupees, 999 shells, full life of hearts, all the weapons, etc, and I am at the highest point of the map above the clouds in the sky.

The end boss on this level is really rock solid. Especially when you have to pass about 30 monsters to get to it each time you die from it.

Anyway back to the game boy itself... I really like my game boy, however there are a few things I think Nintendo missed out when designing this little gadget.

  1. Inferred - The old ones used to have it, why doesn't this one? It would seriously save on damn link cables.
  2. A catch - There is no catch when the lid is closed, so it can come open on its own if its in your bag for example.
  3. A button lock - A great idea would be to have a catch where when lid is closed, the buttons would lock, this feature would be fantastic as the trigger buttons (L and R) can still be used even if the lid is closed
  4. Screen off - The screen should turn off when the lid is closed (although there is a black light on/off button), this would save power.
  5. Head/ear phone jack socket - It seems that you cannot power the GBA SP while you listen to the sound on your headphones, this is rather annoying, I am sure they could have easily implemented this.

I hope Nintendo read this and realise some of the features they are missing. It really strikes me as odd that even the new GBA that has yet to be released in the UK does not have some of these features either, even though it is excessively advanced in terms of technology with its 2x screens one being touch screen. It seems like Nintendo have simply missed the basics.
