Reverse Engineering

Reverse Engineering is the professional term used in the industry for "cracking". This is a process of reverse engineering the software to find out how it works so you can crack it or get a serial number. There are many methods of doing this, there is no one set method, which is why I will not go into it in depth.

In most cases reverse engineering is illegal, as without the authors permission you are not allowed to reverse engineer a peice of software. However members of cracking groups do this anyway. Most of them reverse engineer in a code called ASM (you can find out more information about ASM on Iczelion's Win32 Assembly Homepage. There are some more cracking tutorials located at exetools.com. You can find more tools at ProTools.

Social Engineering

Sometimes it takes a bit of thought to get what you need, and a bit of skill, this is done by so called social engineering methods.

Examples of this would include:

  • Writing a lovely letter (not email) to the creator explaining to them how you purchased the software and there was no serial inside and you are very disapointed.
  • Write a heart warming letter (not email) to the creator explaining that you are currently only a student and you are their biggest fan, and would love to use their software but simply cannot afford to use it.
  • Go to a computer store and inconspicuously open a box (try to avoid sealed boxes) and write down the serial number.
  • Sometimes you can find a serial number from the publisher on their website, but very rarly, maybe if you explain to them that you forgot your password they would be more obliged to provide you with a "replacement".
  • Tell your friend that you lost your serial number and can you have his.

Learn more about Social Engineering.
